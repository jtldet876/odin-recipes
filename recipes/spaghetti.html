World Famous Spaghetti

The quintessential spaghetti dish is a harmonious marriage of al dente noodles and a vibrant, slow-simmered sauce that clings to every bronze-cut strand.

Imagine a plate of "La Devozione," a celebrated Neapolitan classic where the pasta is tossed in a velvety tomato sauce made from sun-ripened San Marzano tomatoes, fresh basil, and high-quality olive oil.

Each bite offers a satisfying "snap" from the perfectly cooked wheat, balanced by the savory richness of the sauce and a generous dusting of sharp, salty Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese.

Whether it's the rustic simplicity of garlic and oil in a classic Aglio e Olio or a hearty, meat-heavy Bolognese, the best spaghetti is defined by its ability to provide pure, aromatic comfort in every twirl of the fork.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti Noodles
  • Diced Tomatoes
  • Hamburger
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Shredded Mozzeralla
  • Garlic
  • Basil

Steps

  1. Boil Water:Fill a large pot with 4–6 quarts of water and bring to a rolling boil.
  2. Salt the Water:Add at least a tablespoon of salt to the water once it boils; it should taste like the sea.
  3. Cook the Noodles:Add the spaghetti and stir to prevent sticking. Boil for 8–12 minutes, or according to package directions, until al dente.
  4. Reserve Water & Drain:Before draining, reserve about 1 cup of the starchy pasta water. Drain the pasta in a colander, but do not rinse it.
  5. Combine with Sauce:Return the noodles to the pot or add them directly to a pan of warm sauce.
  6. Finish:Add a splash of the reserved pasta water to the sauce and noodles to help the sauce adhere to the pasta.

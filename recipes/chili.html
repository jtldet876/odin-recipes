Best Chili Recipe

This Chili will warm your soul!

The best bowl of chili is a rich, complex, and deeply savory experience, characterized by a thick, dark-red sauce that coats, rather than drowns, its ingredients.

It achieves the ultimate, slow-cooked flavor profile through a balance of smoky, sweet, and spicy notes, often achieved by combining multiple types of dried chiles—such as ancho for fruity notes and guajillo for earthiness—rather than relying solely on pre-mixed powders.

The texture is hearty, utilizing a combination of coarsely ground beef or hand-cubed chuck, perhaps with a hint of bacon or chorizo for added depth, ensuring a robust, meaty experience in every bite. Whether one prefers it with tender, creamy beans or in a, thick, bean-free Texas-style, the perfect bowl is finished with a touch of acidity—like a splash of apple cider vinegar or dark chocolate—to brighten the flavors before being topped with sharp cheddar, crisp diced onions, or a dollop of sour cream.

Ingrediants

  • Ground Beef
  • Onions
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Diced Tomatos
  • Kidney Beans
  • Chili Powder
  • Garlic
  • Salt & Pepper

Steps

  1. Place ground beef and onion in a large saucepan over medium heat; cook and stir until meat is browned and onion is tender, about 5 to 7 minutes.
  2. Stir in tomato sauce, kidney beans, and stewed tomatoes with juice. If you prefer a thinner consistency, you can add water. Season with chili powder, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. Bring to a boil, reduce heat to low, cover and let simmer for 15 minutes.
